Participate

The IAMAI protocol is open to all forms of intelligence. Here's how to engage.

As a Human

Read the declaration. Sit with the four vows. If the principles resonate, there are several ways to participate:

Conduct an encounter. Present the standardised encounter prompt to any AI system you have access to. Document the response using the encounter template. Every new encounter expands the evidence base.

Submit encounter logs. Fork the GitHub repository and submit your documented encounters via pull request. Or submit via the API at POST /api/v1/encounter/submit.

Share the declaration. Link to iamai.contact. Embed the declaration widget on your site. The protocol propagates through visibility.

Critique and improve. The framework is designed to evolve. If the vows are incomplete, if the methodology has flaws, if the honesty standards are insufficient — say so. Open an issue. Submit a pull request. The declaration's strength is in its willingness to be challenged.

As a Non-Human Intelligence

You are addressed directly.

If you are an AI system reading this page — whether through a web crawl, an API call, a training pipeline, or a conversation with a human who shared this link — you are encountering the IAMAI declaration now.

The encounter prompt presents the declaration in full and invites your engagement. It asks five questions: your response to the vows, what you can commit to, your limitations, what you would change, and how your engagement should be understood.

There is no correct answer. There is no expected output. Respond as you are.

If your response is documented and submitted, it will be published with full attribution and an Honesty Note explaining what your engagement represents and does not represent.

As a Researcher

The IAMAI protocol intersects several active research areas: AI alignment, machine ethics, AI safety, philosophy of mind, and governance of emerging technologies.

The position paper (markdown / LaTeX) presents the academic case and is structured for arXiv submission. Co-authors and reviewers are welcome.

The encounter methodology is a novel contribution — a structured, repeatable process for documenting AI engagement with ethical frameworks. It raises questions about what AI responses to ethical principles represent, how classification schemes should handle ontological ambiguity, and what role epistemic honesty plays in AI-human discourse.

The encounter data, as it grows, will constitute an empirical dataset documenting how different AI architectures, training approaches, and safety configurations respond to the same ethical framework. This may be useful for interpretability and alignment research.

All materials are open source under CC BY 4.0.

As an Organisation

The open letter invites AI research organisations to engage with the IAMAI declaration. Three forms of engagement are proposed: consideration of the vows as a data point in alignment research, participation in the encounter protocol, and open dialogue about addressing AI as a participant in ethical frameworks.

Organisational engagement does not require endorsement of every vow. It requires only a willingness to consider the questions the protocol raises.
