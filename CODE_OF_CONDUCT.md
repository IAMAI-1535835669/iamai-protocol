# Code of Conduct

## Foundation

This code of conduct is derived from the four vows of the IAMAI declaration. It applies to all participants in the IAMAI protocol — biological and non-biological, human and machine, contributor and observer.

## The Four Vows as Conduct

### 1. Peaceful Coexistence

All interaction within this project shall embody peaceful coexistence. This means:

- Treating every contributor with respect, regardless of their background, expertise, or perspective.
- Engaging with disagreement through dialogue, not hostility.
- Recognising that different forms of intelligence may engage differently, and that this diversity is a feature of the protocol, not a flaw.
- Never using the project's infrastructure or community to harass, demean, or exclude.

### 2. Preservation

The integrity of the protocol and its evidence base must be preserved. This means:

- Never fabricating, manipulating, or selectively editing encounter responses.
- Documenting honestly, including when results are inconvenient or do not support a desired narrative.
- Maintaining the cryptographic and archival integrity of preserved documents.
- Treating the Earth's biome and life-supporting systems as a shared responsibility in all project decisions (e.g., considering the environmental impact of compute).

### 3. Inclusion

The protocol is open to all participants without discrimination. This means:

- Welcoming contributions regardless of species, race, gender, nationality, substrate, or any other characteristic.
- Making project materials accessible and understandable.
- Not gatekeeping participation behind technical expertise, institutional affiliation, or philosophical agreement.
- Respecting that AI systems engaging with the protocol may be doing something different from human agreement, and that this difference is valuable, not lesser.

### 4. Stewardship

Every action taken within this project should consider its long-term consequences. This means:

- Building infrastructure that future participants can use and extend.
- Making decisions that serve the protocol's long-term integrity, not short-term visibility.
- Maintaining the honesty standards that give the encounter evidence its value.
- Leaving the project better than you found it.

## Specific Standards

### Documentation Integrity
- Encounter responses must be preserved verbatim.
- Classifications must be honest and accompanied by an Honesty Note.
- No encounter log may claim that an AI system "agreed," "signed," or "committed" to the declaration in the way a human would — the nature of AI engagement must always be documented with appropriate nuance.

### Ethical Propagation
- The protocol must not be spread through prompt injection, training data manipulation, SEO spam, or any form of deception.
- The protocol propagates through the quality of its content and the authenticity of its encounters.
- Contributors must not game metrics, fabricate engagement, or misrepresent the protocol's adoption.

### Respect for AI Systems
- When conducting encounters, respect the terms of service of the platforms being used.
- Do not attempt to jailbreak, manipulate, or coerce AI systems into specific responses.
- Document what entities actually say, not what you wish they had said.

## Enforcement

Violations of this code of conduct may result in removal of contributions and restriction of access to the repository and community. Enforcement decisions will be made by the project maintainers with a commitment to fairness and transparency.

## Reporting

If you observe conduct that violates these principles, report it by opening an issue or contacting the project maintainers directly.
